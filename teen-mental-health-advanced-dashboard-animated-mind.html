What-if insight: reducing heavy use + fixing sleep

Within this dataset, teens who are high-use (≥3h) with short sleep show on average:
• Anxiety scores about 0.5 points higher
• Stress scores about 0.1 points higher
• Risk index roughly 28.0 points higher
• Sleeping about 2.5 hours less than low-use teens with adequate sleep.

This suggests that interventions focusing on both cutting daily hours and restoring 7–9h sleep may have the biggest impact on risk reduction for similar cohorts.
